# Rules for Arms Race

## (V) (aka the minigame version)

### 

Arms Race is a modded Minecraft gamemode centered around gathering resources and crafting weaponry in order to defend your team’s base from attack, while also launching your own assaults on the other team’s base. Easy, right?

The rules can get a little more complicated, but if you’re new here, that’s why we try to have a balance of experienced and less experienced players on either team. You don’t need to read everything here to be able to play, but knowing a few things about what you’re getting into ahead of time couldn’t hurt.

Don’t worry if you’ve never played before, even if you’ve never even tried modded Minecraft\! As long as you already know the basics of ‘Vanilla’ Minecraft (aka unmodded / normal), and have maybe at some point played at least one FPS game at all, you’re probably more prepared than you’d think.

## Basic Rules

- There are two teams, Red and Blue. The members of each team will be decided randomly at the start of each game.   
  (...But we’ll be sure to balance them in some cases, to keep things fair / interesting)

- When the game starts, everyone will decide on a ‘Grace Period’ length, which is a set amount of time before combat begins in full.  
  (This can be anywhere from 90 minutes to 7 minutes, just depending on how everyone’s feeling)  
  (During this period, no one is allowed to venture outside of their ‘home’ island zone)

- Before the grace period is over, your team will want to ‘arm’ yourselves with weapons and gear, which you’ll do with some good ol’ Mining-and-Crafting.  
  (Everyone starts out with a pretty strong pickaxe, so the ‘mining’ part should be a little easier)  
  (Typically, each team will divide up tasks based on what everyone can do / wants to do, starting with resource gathering, and then splitting up depending on what weapons / gear everyone on the team wants to focus on making)

- The game ends when all 3 beacons of either team have been destroyed, so you’ll want to protect them above all else.  
  (They’re pretty sturdy, each one behind some pretty strong fencing \+ reinforced glass, so nothing short of a nuke\* could take them out at range) (\*other than a ‘thermobaric’, the bomb 1 tier below a nuke)  
  (Even with your starting pickaxe, breaking one should take 5-10 seconds at minimum, meaning going for one while being actively pursued is unlikely to work)

- KeepInventory is enabled, so don’t worry about losing your items when you die. However, death still comes at a cost, as there is a respawn timer in place.   
  (The duration can vary, but it tends to be around 15 seconds)  
  (Anytime you respawn, you’ll be put in a room with one path out, and when you hit the pressure plate at the end of that path you’ll be spawned back at your base properly.)  
  (Breaking things in these rooms (which isn’t easy, as you’re in Gamemode 1\) is strictly disallowed.) (affected methods include explosives, lag, etc.)  
  (*...although bypassing them through certain means is technically permissible*)  
  (...you just may not be able to break any blocks, until you respawn again *properly*)

- We use a mod called “NoDamI”, short for ‘No Damage Invincibility’.   
  This mod makes it so that, unlike in Minecraft normally, there is *no cooldown* between taking damage multiple times.   
  (And since we’re in old Minecraft combat (pre-1.9, so no cooldown on swinging), this means melee combat generally results in an instant kill, or death, one way or another)  
  (...This isn’t that big of a deal, though, because most combat in this game is ranged, anyway. You’ve got guns, remember? …Or should have some eventually, anyway)  
  (The reason we have this mod is so that rapid-fire weapons, like assault rifles or gun turrets, can hit people properly and not be *completely useless* compared to lower-fire-rate weapons like snipers and the sort)

- …And yes, Friendly Fire is off. No trigger discipline needed here\!  
  (…Except for explosives, or running people over with cars, or wrecking air vehicles, and maybe a few other things. …But for *most* weapons and purposes, Friendly Fire should be impossible.)

- No bridging\! This isn’t Bedwars, or Skywars, or Cake Wars, or Cupcake Wars, or Storage Wars, or any of that crap. *We* have PLANES\! And helicopters, and cars, and stuff. Also guns, so even if you were allowed, it wouldn’t be easy to pull off.

- Overall, just don’t do anything stupid. If it seems OP, it probably is, and that’s no fun for the other team. The majority of this gamemode’s rules are based on honor, because in Minecraft version 1.7 (which this modpack uses), there aren’t enough commands available to hardcode most of this stuff (not that it’d be entirely possible in 1.12, either, tbf). Just don’t do anything that seems wrong, and ask people first whenever you’re not sure.

## General Game Info

#### Prep Time

- Each team’s base starts with some basic structures, like small pens for cows, sheep, and chickens, a small seared smeltery, and assembly boxes for armor and certain guns. (as well as some defensive structures and turrets, but you won’t need those until later.)  
- You can’t cross the land-bridge towards the rest of the map until prep time is up, so you’ll just be doing things around your home island for the time being.   
- Mining should take up a large amount of everyone’s starting time. When you don’t know what to do, mining is always worthwhile. You’ll want to gather any ores you find, as most of them will be useful in one way or another. Also, because the map consists of floating islands, remember that all ores spawn at all y-levels. Also, later on in prep time, whoever is on Miner duty should use a Mining Hammer, as it’s significantly faster and more efficient than a normal pickaxe.  
  - Gold is the most important to look out for. It’s notoriously rare, and necessary for starting development of machinery. (Garrett, the main mapmaker, claims that he set gold spawn rates to be “The same as Vanilla”, but nobody really believes this.) (“I will show you the file you \[CENSORED\] little \[REDACTED\]” \- Garrett)  
  - Iron is needed for practically everything, and you can never have enough of it, regardless of how many stacks of ore or how much you can duplicate it with machines.  
  - Coal is needed for fuel (not just for furnaces, but cars and vehicles too), as well as a component in making steel, which you’ll need for a lot of things.  
  - Redstone is needed for a few things, most notably improvement of tools from Tinker’s Construct, like the mining hammer and the legendary crossbow. Another resource you can never have too much of.  
  - Copper and Tin are pretty niche. You’ll need a little copper for a few machines and materials, and tin for one or two things, but don’t worry about grabbing tin after the first few veins. Copper, however, is used for a few ammo types, so don’t leave it behind.  
  - Osmium is great for pickaxes and basic tools, as well as a few machines, so it’s mostly worthwhile. (If you don’t start with an obsidian one, it’s what you’ll want to use after stone instead of iron, as it’s objectively better.)  
  - Aluminum is only needed for a few things in Tinker’s Construct, so after the first few veins it probably isn’t worth carrying all of it.  
      
  - Yellorite is only used for Big Reactors, so if your team isn’t planning on building a nuclear reactor this game, just ignore it. (No, it’s not gold, unfortunately, despite how yellow it is. This is one of the many benefits of ‘Waila’, or “What Am I Looking At”, the mod that shows you just that up at the top of your screen.)  
  - Sulfur is useful for just a few things, primarily explosives, so don’t worry about getting too much of it unless your team’s planning on making a lot.  
  - Diamonds, obviously, are always worth getting. (Surprisingly, though, they aren’t as useful as usual. For one, you aren’t allowed to make armor out of it, and the tools are outclassed by many others. They’re pretty much only used for advanced machinery, a few vehicles, and the Teleporter, which is a very expensive but potentially game-changing device.)  
- You should also have a Farmer to watch / make / upgrade some farms. Your team will probably want to make at least an expanded sheep farm, a sugarcane farm, and a chicken farm. (Plus moving some of them underground. None of the farms have to be underground, but moving them there can help to protect your resources from air raids later on.)  
- Farms you’ll want to keep in mind:   
  - Sheep are needed for armor (We typically only allow Flan’s mod basic armors, which take dyed wool, meaning that you’ll want someone overseeing the sheep pens if you want some protection in battle.) (Because sheep need space for grass, making a larger pen should be a priority.)  
  - Chickens and sugarcane for crossbows (feathers are needed for bolts, and sugarcane for paper components) (sugarcane can also be used for gunpowder, but there are other ways to get it that are more reliable)  
  - Wheat for animal breeding and food (remember: for bread, SAG-Mill wheat for flour, smelt the flour into bread, then smelt the bread again for toast, which is slightly better at almost no cost.)  
  - Melons, also for food (Everyone starts with some bacon for food, but use them sparingly, as they won’t last forever. Always remember to use a juicer on melons before eating them, though, as melon juice is more filling than the melons themselves... for some reason)   
  - Cows are used for milk, sometimes leather (you can use milk with snow and a few food prep tools to make ice cream, snow is available from the mountain islands. Both of the main ingredients here are effectively infinite, so this is the best source of food available.)  
  - Also, there are magical dye-plants that you’ll find near the spawn area. You can gather these for the dyes needed to make armor, but *make sure not to step on them*. *You have been warned*.  
- There are also a few more advanced things your team will want to focus on:  
  - Engineer(s) for machinery development (Putting together power production, ore doubling, and gunpowder grinders, as well as many other complicated electrical mekanisms and systems)  
  - A Blacksmith for smeltery usage (Crafting more complex tools, such as Mining Hammers, Excavation Shovels, and Lumber Axes, which are basically heavy / AoE versions of their respective normal tools. Also a few weapons, like longswords that let you perform long-jumps, or more importantly Crossbows, my favorite weapon)  
- But those each would take entire sections of their own to explain, so just know that these two professions  alongside the previous two should be the typical roles for everyone on the team to take. They shouldn’t be rigid, swapping out jobs and helping out others when needed, but making sure that everyone is contributing is always important.

#### Combat

- When the prep timer is up, anyone can cross the land bridges and access the rest of the map, including the other team’s base. Thus, combat has truly begun.  
- The map itself consists of a central path between the two bases, crossing through a mountain on both sides and a sand dune in the center, which we refer to as “mid”, “middle”, or anything like that.  
- For easy communication, we refer to the land bridges as “bridge (1-4)”, where bridge 1 is the first you’d cross while leaving your base, and 4 is the one after the enemy mountain that leads to theirs. So if someone says “Enemy on bridge 1”, that means you may want to consider falling back to defend if someone isn’t already there.  
- Earlygame, you’ll want to rush “Treasure Island”, the gold island between the two bases, to get a hold of the gold and resources there and prevent the other team from doing so. If you don’t have many weapons or people ready, though, it’s not detrimental to wait a bit before heading out. Not everyone considers it as much of a priority, especially if you have enough resources for machines already. Bear in mind that the only way to reach it is by air, so any excursion to the island can be dangerous.  
- Typically, both teams end up with relatively similar weapons. These can come and go as we add new ones or rework old ones, but you’ll always want to be ahead of the game on ammo and weapon production. Here are a few that we’ve seen so far:   
- Assault Rifles: These haven’t been used in a while, as snipers can tend to be more resource-efficient, but used correctly they can be very difficult to evade in short- and mid-range, and make for easy kill-confirms.   
  - AUG (Flan’s): This was found to be the most viable after a few tests, due to its higher damage and clip capacity for the resource cost, especially with an added foregrip and sight  
  - Other assault rifles, however, are available. (Nobody uses them yet, but feel free to try some out)  
- Sniper Rifles: These… would practically be the whole game if it weren’t for the next two, honestly. Typically they can kill in one hit if you get a headshot, or two otherwise, and at a range near render distance. The real name of the game is aim, though, as none of these are hitscan, and few hold a linear trajectory.   
  - Barrett .50 Cal (Flan’s): used to be the most popular, even without its game-breaking explosive ammo cartridges  
  - L96 (Flan’s): After testing (and my insistence for weapon variation), we found these to be more accurate than the other Flan’s rifles, though they deal slightly less damage   
  - Dragunov (Flan’s): This one is more fun at a slightly shorter range, with its increased fire rate and decreased reload time, but a little less damage (still 1hit on HS tho)  
  - Barrett (MW): Currently the most popular, due to its combination of high damage and range, and low bullet drop, in addition to a scope you can add that makes it much easier to use  
  - Gewehr (MW): Relatively cheap but powerful sniper that can one shot. Popular for its cheap ammo.   
- Crossbows: Love them or hate them, they’re fun to use, and break up the lineup of basic FPS weapons for a little spice. We use the ones from Tinker’s Construct, as they’re the only weapon from the mod that is comparably powerful to a gun. Currently, there are two types that people use, and one type of bolt (all of which I prototyped in singleplayer, because it’s a very... complicated mod)  
  - Steel Crossbow: This one is comparable to a sniper rifle, just without a scope or headshots. It can reach near render distance with proper angling, maybe just a little shorter range than a rifle, but it can work effectively at any range. It has a \~90% chance to one-shot, regardless of hit location, but its downside is that it takes over 4 seconds to reload.  
  - Wood Crossbow: This one, despite a limb like a traditional bow, is actually comparable to an assault rifle. With enough modification, it can fire around 3 bolts per second, and they deal enough damage that you only need 2-3 hits to kill. Their only downside, aside from relatively low durability, is that the bolts have significant drop, and only reach roughly midrange in addition to being harder to aim in some cases.

- Later on you’ll start using vehicles, including cars for transport, planes for raids or support, helicopters for airlifts, or even tanks sometimes. These are a little harder to explain, though, and their controls are… complex, to say the least. I’d suggest riding shotgun until you can have someone teach you about them. (unless the driver happens to be Josh, in which case you’ll often be better off walking) (see “Advanced Tactics” if you want to try to learn these, anyway)  
- Also, it may be worth learning to use a turret.   
  - The typical turrets in your base are AA turrets, or Anti-Aircraft. You’ll most commonly be able to use these against aircraft, like planes or helicopters, but in a pinch they can still hit ground vehicles or players.  
  - Turrets are much easier than the other vehicles in that you don’t have to move or fuel them. For all turrets, you simply hold right click to fire, and look around as you would normally to aim. You can press R to open the inventory and reload ammunition, which takes iron and gunpowder. Also, there is a red bar that shows overheating, which will stop you from firing when it hits the end and will start a cooldown period.

## Additional Rules

- Do not place a Mekanism teleporter in enemy territory. (Teleporters are dangerous, but we’ve found that allowing them up through mid to be decently fair, as otherwise they would be much harder to defend against.) (As for hiding it, pretty much anything is fair game, but keep in mind it’ll likely be found eventually regardless.)  
- When using an air vehicle, dismounting is only allowed up to the enemy mountain. (‘Airdropping’ on the enemy’s main island is strictly disallowed, as it was very infuriating during the few games it was allowed before)  
- Do not break blocks that are not exposed to the sky in enemy territory. This includes the mesa walls, the hills on the main island, and even on the land bridges. You can dig a hole, but not an underground tunnel. (This is considered ‘tunneling’, and it’s really annoying to deal with as a defender, so please try not to) (...I know this goes against a lot of core instincts in Minecraft, but it’s just no fun if you aren’t even given a chance to see an attacker coming at all)  
- No using F3+B (anymore) (if you don’t know what this means don’t worry about it)   
  (F3+A is still allowed, since it’s more balanced (only works properly at a considerable distance), and can happen inadvertently using the edge of your render distance anyway)

### Banned Items

Banned due to gamemode:

- Beds (can circumvent respawn points and timers)  
- Nether Portals (can circumvent normal travel paths too easily)  
- Jetpacks, Gliders, Flan’s Parachutes (can circumvent normal travel paths too easily) (MCHeli Parachutes still work)  
- Any armors other than basic Flan’s gear (traditionally we use the German armor, as it’s the easiest to make and the best general camo) (the weapon meta is currently balanced around the maximum of 8 defense points that these armors give, allowing anything more than that would make certain weapons (crossbows) significantly more powerful)  
- Picking up or repairing a vehicle/turret in the middle of combat (meaning while there are enemies around that you are aware of, or while your base is being attacked. It should be pretty self-explanatory, but it’s just so that you can’t just keep reusing the same ones, or in the case of repairing, so you can’t get an effectively-invincible tank with a teammate playing pocket-healer with a wrench)

Banned because they’re just too powerful (from experience):

- Riot Shields (A Flan’s mod item that basically negates all flan’s mod gun damage (except piercing), which is dumb)  
- Explosive Barrett Ammo (An alt-ammo for the Flan’s mod Barrett .50 cal., which makes each bullet practically one-shot anyone and/or anything, in addition to doing terrain and AoE damage, for the cost of just a bit more gunpowder)  
- Javelin Missile Top-Attack Mode (An alt-fire mode for MCHeli’s Javelin Missile Launcher, which makes its missiles fly upwards and then straight down on top of the target, bypassing any effective method of counter (walls, and/or interception via shooting) while also potentially hiding the attacker’s position)

Banned Crossbow Stuff (AKA Max’s Nerfs):

- Leaf Fletching (Effectively triples ammo count, and is significantly easier to get than feathers, with the only downside of ‘-10% accuracy’ (which I’m pretty sure doesn’t even do anything in this version). This way, you *have* to make a chicken farm (or at least pay any attention to chickens) if you want to make crossbows)  
- Bolt Modifiers (Not super significant, mostly just means you can’t use moss for passive ammo regen, or quartz for additional damage)  
- Plastic (Should be removed from the map now, but in case it ever isn’t, no more plastic crossbows. One time was enough (for some people :p ))
